## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- GPT-4o's retirement is a useful calibration point for the archive. The model was released in May 2024 and is legacy by February 2026 — 21 months. If that obsolescence cycle holds, today's "frontier" models (GPT-5.3, Claude Sonnet 4.6) will be legacy by late 2027. Anyone building on specific model capabilities rather than abstracted interfaces is taking on technical debt with a known expiry date.
- The China CCTV robot showcase is the clearest geopolitical signal since Google's £5B UK investment (W38). State television in front of hundreds of millions of viewers is not a product launch — it's a national capability statement. The Sputnik analogy is apt: the significance is in the demonstration, not the current capability level. This will accelerate Western physical AI investment.
- Anthropic's Super Bowl self-mocking ads are worth noting as a brand strategy inflection. Every other AI company markets capability and aspiration. Anthropic marketed humour and self-awareness. The Top 10 app store result suggests this resonates with the consumer market in a way that capability claims no longer do — consistent with the W49 observation that novelty is gone and trust is the new currency.
- OpenAI models contributing to theoretical physics is a landmark even if the specific results are narrow. "AI synthesises existing knowledge" is a tool; "AI generates new knowledge in physics" is a different category of capability. If reproducible and peer-verified, this is the kind of result that will look important in retrospect regardless of whether it generates immediate headlines.
- The "Assistant Axis" framework (autonomy, reliability, human oversight) is Anthropic's attempt to create a structured evaluation language for AI agent behaviour — something that doesn't exist as a standard yet. Given the W06 OpenAI alignment team dissolution, having an independent lab publishing evaluation frameworks becomes more important, not less.
