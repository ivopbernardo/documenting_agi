# Week 11, 2026 — Mar 09–15

> **Key Takeaway:** The Pentagon deal fallout continues producing concrete consequences: OpenAI's robotics lead resigned on principle after the DoD deployment announcement. AMI Labs (LeCun) raised $1.03B — reportedly the largest seed round in European startup history — signalling that the "beyond transformers" bet has serious capital behind it. And both Claude and ChatGPT launched inline interactive visualisations in the same week: the race to be the best visual explainer is now officially on.

---

## 📰 News

- **OpenAI robotics lead Caitlin Kalinowski resigns over Pentagon deal** — Built OpenAI's robotics hardware team; resigned on principle after OpenAI announced deployment of its models on classified DoD networks. Third major safety-related departure following the alignment team dissolution (W06) and the mission statement change (W08). *(Mar 2026)*
- **Meta acquires Moltbook** — The AI-only social network (W05) acquired by Meta. Partly a consolation prize after losing the OpenClaw creator to OpenAI. Adds contributors to Meta's superintelligence team. *(Mar 2026)*
- **Legora raises $550M at $5.5B valuation** — Scandinavian legal AI platform; Series D to fund US expansion. Legal AI moving from cautious pilots to full enterprise deployment. *(Mar 2026)*
- **NVIDIA invests $2B in Nebius** — European AI cloud infrastructure company spun out of Yandex. NVIDIA expanding its infrastructure investment outside US hyperscalers. *(Mar 2026)*
- **Adobe losing 34% of value over past year** — Context for the Adobe AI Assistant launch: the company is under significant investor pressure as SaaS-pocalypse fears mount. AI features are both the threat and the attempted defence. *(Mar 2026)*
- **AMI Labs raises $1.03B seed round** — Yann LeCun's Paris-based world-models startup. Reportedly the largest seed round ever raised by a European startup. The "beyond transformers" research cluster (AMI Labs, World Labs, Project Prometheus) now has serious capital. *(Mar 2026)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- Caitlin Kalinowski's resignation is the most concrete internal consequence of the DoD deal yet. The alignment team dissolution (W06) was an organisational restructuring; a named executive who built a core team leaving on principle is a values signal that's harder to reframe. The cumulative picture across W06, W08, W09, W10, W11: OpenAI is losing its safety-oriented staff while expanding its government and military footprint.
- OpenAI acquiring Promptfoo — a red-teaming and security platform — in the same month it signed a DoD deal without Anthropic's ethical guardrails is a notable juxtaposition. It could be genuine safety investment; it could be reputational cover. The two interpretations are not mutually exclusive.
- AMI Labs' $1.03B seed is a landmark for European AI investment as much as for the world-models thesis. Paris is becoming a real AI research hub (alongside Mistral's presence), and the capital flowing into non-transformer architectures now totals several billion across AMI, World Labs, and physical AI startups. If transformers hit a ceiling, the next wave is funded.
- Claude and ChatGPT launching inline visualisations in the same week is the latest instance of the simultaneous-release pattern (W05: Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.3 Codex 20 minutes apart). The competitive surveillance between labs is now tight enough that major product features launch in parallel rather than sequentially.
- The 140 million weekly math students using AI (stat of the week) is worth holding alongside the OECD -17% exam performance finding (W05) and the MIT brain study (W04). Scale of beneficial access is real; cognitive dependency risk is also real. Both are true simultaneously.
- Legora at $5.5B in legal AI confirms what the Harvey partnership in the Claude Marketplace (W10) suggested: enterprise law is past the pilot phase. Legal is one of the highest-value, highest-stakes, most documentation-heavy domains — exactly where domain-specific AI with strong audit trails has the clearest ROI.
