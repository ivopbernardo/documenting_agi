# Week 04, 2026 — Jan 19–25

> **Key Takeaway:** The Anthropic Economic Index gives the most detailed picture yet of how AI is moving through the economy — enterprise API use is 75% automation-heavy, Office & Admin tasks reached 13% of API usage (+3pp in 3 months). Simultaneously, MIT's "Your Brain on ChatGPT" study is the first neurological signal of the second-order cost: people who outsource writing to AI show lower neural connectivity and can't remember the content of their own essays. The question is no longer "what can AI do?" but "what is it quietly training us to stop doing?"

## 📰 News

- **Apple chooses Google Gemini to power next-generation Siri** — Apple reportedly explored Anthropic but the multibillion-dollar annual cost and OpenAI's competitive positioning pushed them toward Google's cloud stack. Siri becomes a full-blown chatbot this year. Google gains a 2B+ device distribution channel; Apple gains frontier model capability without building it. *(Jan 2026)*
- **Anthropic doubles funding target to $20B** — Round now targeting $20B (up from $10B, W01) toward a ~$350B valuation, per FT/TechCrunch. Investor interest in AI infrastructure continues to climb with no apparent ceiling. *(Jan 2026)*
- **Yann LeCun officially launches AMI Labs** — Startup focused on world models that understand and reason about the real world beyond language. Backed by top AI researchers. Explicitly bets against transformer-based architectures as the primary driver of next-generation AI breakthroughs. *(Jan 2026)*
- **Anthropic Economic Index (Jan 15, 2026)** — Most detailed view yet of AI's movement through the economy. Key findings: top 10 tasks account for 24% of consumer usage and 32% of enterprise API usage; coding still dominant but enterprise expanding into back-office (Office & Admin +3pp to 13% of API usage in 3 months); consumer use is 52% augmentation vs 45% automation; enterprise API is ~75% automated/directive; US state-level AI adoption inequality falling fast — parity possible in 2–5 years; global adoption remains strongly correlated with GDP per capita. *(Jan 2026)*
- **AI matches average human creativity on some tests** — Large study (100,000+ humans vs current AI models): GPT-4 can outperform the average person on certain creativity measures. Top 10% of human imaginative thinkers still maintain a clear lead. *(Jan 2026)*

## 📄 Research Papers

- **MIT Media Lab: "Your Brain on ChatGPT"** — EEG scans comparing manual writers vs AI-first writers. Key findings: manual writers show higher neural interconnectivity; AI-primary users show less coordinated neural effort and lower content recall for their own essays. Core mechanism: if you don't struggle with the first draft, the brain doesn't map the information. First neurological study of AI's cognitive second-order effects. *(Jan 2026)*
- **Anthropic Economic Index** — Detailed economic analysis of Claude usage patterns across consumer and enterprise. Covers task concentration, augmentation vs automation ratios, geographic adoption inequality. [Full report](https://www.anthropic.com) *(Jan 2026)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- Apple choosing Google Gemini over Anthropic or OpenAI is a distribution earthquake. Google gains a default position on 2B+ Apple devices — the exact distribution moat that W42's ChatGPT plateau analysis said was the next competitive frontier. Apple gets the capability; Google gets the reach. OpenAI and Anthropic don't get either.
- The Anthropic Economic Index's 75% enterprise automation figure is the most important economic data point this week. When three-quarters of enterprise API interactions are directive/automated (not human-augmented), AI is no longer a tool people use — it's a process component running without human input. The W43 and W49 supervision paradox concerns become structural at that ratio.
- The MIT brain connectivity study is the year's most important non-commercial research finding. The mechanism is specific and measurable: no cognitive struggle = no memory encoding = no retained knowledge. This isn't speculation about AI making people lazy — it's an EEG measurement of what's actually happening in the brain during AI-assisted writing. The implications for education and expertise development are significant.
- LeCun's AMI Labs officially launching makes the "beyond transformers" research bet a funded, staffed reality rather than a theoretical position. Whether AMI Labs produces a genuine alternative or validates the transformer approach by failing to beat it, the existence of a well-funded counter-bet accelerates clarity on the question.
- The creativity benchmark result (GPT-4 beats the average human, top 10% still lead) is a useful frame for what AI displacement actually looks like in cognitive work: it doesn't eliminate human value uniformly — it raises the floor while compressing the middle. Average performers face more displacement pressure than exceptional ones.
