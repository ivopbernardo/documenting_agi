## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- Anthropic at $350B valuation (if the round closes) would make it worth more than most Fortune 100 companies — a company less than three years old with no public financials. The capital is real; the question is whether the enterprise revenue trajectory (W45: $70B by 2028 projection) can justify it.
- The Manus acquisition geopolitics story is important because it signals that AI agents — not just models — are now treated as dual-use technology with export control implications. Singapore-based Manus being acquired by Meta puts it under US regulatory jurisdiction, which China interprets as a threat to its own agent development. This won't be the last such dispute.
- Nvidia's 10x inference cost reduction with Rubin changes the economics of every inference-heavy product. If the claims hold at scale, it shifts the constraint from cost to imagination — what would you build if inference were 10x cheaper?
