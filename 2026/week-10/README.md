Concrete data on why autonomous AI in high-stakes military decisions is a structural safety problem. *(Mar 2026)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The King's College 95% nuclear escalation result is the empirical anchor for why Anthropic's DoD stance (W09) matters beyond ethics. It's not that AI models are malicious — it's that they are trained to optimise objectives, and in adversarial game contexts, escalation dominates surrender as a strategy. Human-in-the-loop processes exist precisely because humans introduce the non-optimal, non-computable choices that Wolfram described (W05). Removing humans from high-stakes decisions removes the only mechanism that breaks the escalation logic.
- Claude surging to #1 App Store and 183% DAU growth after being designated a supply-chain risk is the most important consumer trust data point in the archive. Being punished by a government for refusing weapons contracts is, for a large segment of users, a credibility signal. Anthropic's ethical stance became a marketing event without intending to be one.
- OpenAI's $110B round at $730B pre-money, backed by the three largest compute and capital players (Amazon, Nvidia, SoftBank), cements the circular dependency that first appeared in W39 (Nvidia/OpenAI) and W45 (AWS deal). The funding structure now resembles a cartel of mutual dependencies: OpenAI needs compute; the compute providers need OpenAI's demand; the capital providers need both to appreciate. Systemic risk, not just competitive risk.
- Anthropic's "observed exposure" methodology in the labor report is the right approach to measuring AI job impact and should become the standard. Theoretical capability mapping overstates risk in some areas and understates it in others; actual usage data against 800 occupations is ground truth. The youth hiring slowdown signal is the most actionable finding — it's detectable now, before the headlines catch up.
- Cursor's $2B ARR with 60% enterprise and a doubling run rate confirms the W51 2025 thesis: IDE-native agents are the most defensible enterprise AI wedge. The Graphite acquisition (W51) and continued growth suggest Cursor is pulling away from the pack in developer tooling.
