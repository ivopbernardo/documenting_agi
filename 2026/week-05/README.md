# Week 05, 2026 — Jan 26–Feb 01

> **Key Takeaway:** One of the densest weeks in the archive. Claude Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.3 Codex launched 20 minutes apart — the model war is now real-time. Microsoft is pulling back AI from Windows after user backlash. The "dead web" is becoming measurable: bots now account for a significant share of all web traffic. ## 📰 News

- **OECD Digital Education Outlook 2026** — Students who gained access to AI tools performed -17% on written exams without AI support. Confirms the MIT brain connectivity finding (W04) at population scale: dependency is forming faster than educators can respond. *(Feb 2026)*
- **Microsoft scales back Copilot and Recall in Windows 11** — User feedback forced a pivot; Microsoft vowing to improve AI UX in Windows. Significant reversal after years of aggressive AI integration into every corner of Windows. The first major Big Tech product rollback driven by user rejection of AI features. *(Feb 2026)*
- **Moltbook: the social network only AI agents can join** — Reddit-like site where only AI bots post and interact; humans can observe but not participate. The "Moltys" debate philosophy and cryptocurrency. Simultaneous data: AI bots now account for a significant share of all web traffic (Wired). The "dead web" hypothesis is becoming measurable reality. *(Feb 2026)*
- **ElevenLabs raises $500M at $11B valuation** — Sequoia-backed round. ElevenLabs positioning as the foundational layer for synthetic voice and media. *(Feb 2026)*
- **OpenAI invests in Merge Labs** — Embodied AI investment. OpenAI looking to give GPT spatial intelligence for physical-world operation. Continues the physical AI build-out alongside Project Prometheus (W47), Nvidia Cosmos (W02), and DRIVE Alpamayo-R1 (W48). *(Feb 2026)*
- **Google settles voice assistant spying claims for $68M** — Settled claims that Google Assistant was recording users without the "Hey Google" trigger. Privacy cost of always-on AI made concrete. *(Feb 2026)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- Claude Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.3 Codex launching 20 minutes apart is the most visible signal yet that OpenAI and Anthropic are watching each other's release calendars in real time. The model war has become a PR and timing game as much as a technical one. "Verifiable reasoning" (Anthropic) vs "self-building" (OpenAI) are the two different claims staked in the same news cycle.
- Microsoft pulling back Copilot and Recall is the year's most important product management story so far. After billions spent on AI integration, user rejection is forcing a redesign. The lesson: AI features that users didn't ask for and don't find intuitive create backlash regardless of capability. "AI-native" design requires meeting users where they are, not where the product team assumes they should be.
- The OECD -17% exam performance finding, combined with the MIT brain study (W04), forms the year's most important paired finding: AI dependency is forming at measurable speed in both neurological (EEG) and behavioural (exam scores) data. The policy question — how do educational institutions respond — is urgent and largely unanswered.
- Moltbook and the "dead web" data point to a structural shift in what the internet is: a space where AI agents interact with each other at scale, with humans as observers. This has implications for every business model built on the assumption that web traffic = human attention. Ad networks, SEO, content marketing — all premised on humans reading. That premise is eroding.
- GPT-5.3 Codex being "instrumental in building itself" is a milestone worth flagging for the archive. If accurate, it's the first mainstream commercial model with a credible self-referential development claim. The recursive improvement loop — AI making AI better — has been theoretical; this is the first product-level assertion of it.
- ElevenLabs at $11B is the voice equivalent of what Midjourney was for images: a pure-play foundation for a modality. As synthetic voice becomes indistinguishable from human voice, the provenance and watermarking questions raised in W38 (Sora 2 responsible use) apply with equal force.
