# Week 03, 2026 — Jan 12–18

> **Key Takeaway:** The "magic" phase of AI is winding down; the "utility" phase is taking its place. OpenAI introducing ads to ChatGPT's free tier signals the business model is under pressure — infrastructure costs require revenue diversification. Anthropic's Claude Cowork goes the other direction: deep local execution, no ads, agents that work on your files. Two very different bets on how AI becomes sustainable. The novelty of a chatbot that writes poems is gone; the friction of agents that actually do work is finally starting to drop.

---

## 📰 News

- **OpenAI tests ads in ChatGPT** — Advertising coming to ChatGPT's free tier. Positioned as a funding mechanism to offset infrastructure costs and keep AI access broad. Ads clearly labelled, segregated from model outputs; opt-outs and sensitivity filters planned. A fundamental shift in OpenAI's product DNA — and the clearest signal yet that the current revenue model isn't covering costs. *(Jan 2026)*
- **OpenAI and ServiceNow multi-year partnership** — AI agents integrated directly into ServiceNow enterprise workflows (IT operations, customer service). Underscores the commercialisation of AI within core business software. *(Jan 2026)*
- **Slackbot upgraded with Claude capabilities** — Salesforce enhancing Slack's built-in assistant with Anthropic's Claude models. Improves summarisation, knowledge search, and workflow insight inside Slack. AI embedded where work already happens rather than requiring a context switch. *(Jan 2026)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- OpenAI introducing ads is the moment that confirms the underlying unit economics of frontier AI don't yet work at consumer scale. Every business model signal this year — the $830B valuation bid (W51), the $38B AWS deal (W45), the leaked $865M Microsoft payments (W47) — pointed to this. Ads buy time while the enterprise revenue (W44, W50) scales up.
- Claude Cowork vs ChatGPT ads is the clearest strategic divergence in the market right now. Anthropic is betting on deep local execution — agents that own your file system and work environment. OpenAI is betting on mass-market distribution supported by advertising. Both can work, but they target fundamentally different relationships between AI and user.
- The Slackbot/ServiceNow integrations are the enterprise version of the same pattern: AI embedded in existing workflows rather than requiring new habits. The tools that win enterprise aren't the ones with the best benchmarks — they're the ones that require the least behaviour change to deliver value. This aligns directly with the W43 Wharton finding on adoption barriers.
- The AI-Values observation (every model carries implicit values from training data and RLHF) is worth archiving as a first-principles point. As AI embeds into communications (Gmail AI Inbox, W02), work (Claude Cowork, Slackbot), and healthcare (W01/W02), the values baked into these systems scale with adoption. The philosophical question becomes operational.
- The week's through-line is the shift from "AI as impressive demo" to "AI as work infrastructure." Cowork, ServiceNow agents, Slack integration — these aren't features, they're attempts to become load-bearing parts of how organisations operate. That's a much higher bar, and a much larger prize.
