- **Meta "Avocado" frontier model delayed to at least May** — Internal tests showed underperformance vs Gemini 3.0, OpenAI, and Anthropic on reasoning, coding, and writing. Meta's leadership reportedly discussed licensing Google Gemini as a stopgap — asking their chief competitor in ads, video, and smart glasses to cover their AI gap. *(Mar 2026)*
- **Jeff Bezos seeks $100B for Project Prometheus industrial fund** — Fund would acquire industrial companies in chipmaking, aerospace, defence, and automotive manufacturing, then use Prometheus AI models to overhaul their operations. Bezos scaling the physical AI thesis from one startup to an entire industrial acquisition strategy. *(Mar 2026)*
- **Anthropic invests $100M in Claude Partner Network** — Formal programme for consultancies and professional services firms deploying Claude. Partners get technical certifications, dedicated Applied AI engineers, and co-investment eligibility. Enterprise go-to-market infrastructure. *(Mar 2026)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- OpenAI acquiring Astral (uv, Ruff, ty) is the most underappreciated strategic move of the week. These are not AI tools — they're Python toolchain infrastructure used by hundreds of millions of developers. Owning the packaging, linting, and type-checking tools that developers use before they even open an AI assistant means OpenAI touches every Python workflow at the earliest possible stage. This is the deepest developer lock-in play yet, deeper than IDE integration.
- Meta's "Avocado" delay and the Gemini licensing discussion expose a structural problem that no amount of compute spending fixes: frontier model research talent is the constraint, not infrastructure. Meta has the infrastructure (W44 2025: $70B capex). What it apparently lacks is the research output to match. The Yang Song hire (W39 2025) and continued talent raids haven't closed the gap.
- Bezos's $100B industrial fund through Project Prometheus is the most ambitious physical AI bet in the archive. It's not just building AI for industry — it's acquiring the industries themselves to deploy AI into. If successful, it's vertical integration at a scale that makes every previous "AI company" look like a feature, not a platform.
- NVIDIA wrapping NemoClaw around OpenClaw is the enterprise software playbook applied to AI agents: take what the community builds and add the governance, security, and deployment infrastructure that enterprises require. NVIDIA is not trying to compete with OpenClaw on capability — it's trying to own the enterprise distribution of it.
- OpenAI's "automated AI researcher" roadmap (autonomous research intern by September 2026, multi-agent research system by 2028) is the most specific public timeline for recursive AI self-improvement that any major lab has published. This is the capability that, if it works, changes the trajectory of everything else. Worth tracking carefully against the actual September 2026 date.
- The Meta layoff / restructuring pattern (W43 2025 Amazon → W12 Meta) is accelerating. Both companies framed layoffs as "restructuring around AI." The honest version: AI is compressing the marginal value of certain coordination, support, and analytical roles faster than new roles are being defined. The W10 92,000 February job losses figure now has more corporate context.
