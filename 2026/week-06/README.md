# Week 06, 2026 — Feb 02–08

> **Key Takeaway:** Three converging signals this week. OpenAI disbanding its Mission Alignment team while simultaneously rolling out ads in ChatGPT suggests safety is being traded for revenue. Anthropic closes a $30B Series G at $380B valuation with a $14B revenue run-rate — the enterprise lead is real and growing. And Harvard Business Review finds that AI isn't freeing up time; it's enabling "workload creep" — workers now juggle 3–4 parallel AI-assisted threads and burn out by afternoon. AI promised the 4-day week; it's delivering more work, faster.

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- OpenAI disbanding its alignment team in the same week it rolls out ads is the sharpest tension in the archive so far. The alignment team's job was to ensure the model pursues the right goals; the ads team's job is to ensure the model generates revenue. These are not always compatible objectives, and removing the institutional check while adding a commercial incentive is a concerning combination.
- Anthropic's $14B revenue run-rate at $380B valuation is the most important competitive data point of the year so far. It means Anthropic is not just a well-funded research lab — it's a genuine enterprise revenue business. The gap between OpenAI's consumer/ad model and Anthropic's enterprise model is becoming a strategic identity difference, not just a product difference.
- The HBR "workload creep" finding is the counterweight to every productivity stat cited in this archive. The W43 Wharton report showed 40–60 minutes saved per day; the HBR study shows that saved time gets immediately filled with more work rather than leisure or recovery. This is "Jevons paradox" applied to cognitive labour: making thinking cheaper increases the consumption of thinking.
- GPT-5.3 Codex Spark's self-correction speed improvement is a meaningful agentic AI metric. In autonomous coding workflows, the bottleneck isn't generation speed — it's the time the agent spends identifying and fixing its own errors. Halving that time has a larger practical impact than headline benchmark improvements.
- The RLHF repo resource is worth noting in the context of the alignment team dissolution. As OpenAI moves its safety expertise into a "Chief Futurist" role, the practical knowledge of how to build aligned models risks becoming distributed across practitioners rather than concentrated in a dedicated team. That's both a risk and an opportunity — more people understanding RLHF is good; fewer people being paid to focus exclusively on alignment is concerning.
