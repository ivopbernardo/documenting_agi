# Week 6, 2025 — Feb 03–07

> **Key Takeaway:** The open-source vs. proprietary battle intensified on multiple fronts: real-world benchmarks showed models have different strengths (not one winner), Mistral and OpenAI pushed for broader access, and a new paper (s1-32B) beat o1 on key benchmarks while staying open-source. Meanwhile, Google's reversal on military AI ethics added a dark note to the week.

---

## 📰 News

- **OpenAI to release a free AI model** — In response to growing adoption of open-source alternatives (DeepSeek R1, Mistral), OpenAI announced plans to release a free version of its latest model. Details scarce but signals a defensive move to maintain ecosystem dominance. *(Feb 2025)*
- **Google/Alphabet drops AI ethics pledge on military use** — Alphabet rescinded its commitment to avoid weaponized AI applications. Marks a significant shift in corporate AI governance amid rising geopolitical tensions. *(Feb 2025)*
- **Cognida AI raises $15M to tackle enterprise AI deployment** — Funding to address the gap between AI research and production deployment: scalability, infrastructure, and workforce readiness. Similar space to DareData's Gen-OS. *(Feb 2025)*

## 🚀 Model / Product Launches

- **[Mistral Small 3](https://mistral.ai/news/mistral-small-3)** — Lightweight, cost-effective open-source model. Key features: multilingual, advanced reasoning, Apache 2.0 license. Optimized for speed and efficiency; targets enterprises wanting to avoid high compute costs. *(Feb 2025)*

---

## 📄 Research Papers

- **[s1: Simple Test-Time Scaling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.19393)** — Introduces "budget forcing": extend or terminate a model's thinking process based on computational constraints. Key results: up to 27% improvement on competitive math tasks; proposed model s1-32B surpasses OpenAI o1 on key benchmarks while fully open-source. Questions whether brute-force AI reasoning has a ceiling. *(Feb 2025)*
- **[Beyond Benchmarks: DeepSeek R1 vs. o1 on Real-World Tasks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.01718)** — Analysis comparing DeepSeek R1 and o1 on practical (non-benchmark) tasks. Findings: R1 excels at mathematical reasoning and RL; o1 remains superior in generalist capabilities and test-time scaling. Reinforces "the future is multi-model." *(Feb 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- Traditional AI benchmarks are increasingly seen as vanity metrics — real-world task performance diverges significantly from leaderboard rankings.
- Google's reversal on military AI is a bellwether: as geopolitical competition intensifies, corporate AI ethics commitments may be the first casualty.
- Enterprise deployment remains the unsexy bottleneck — the gap between POC and production is where most AI value gets lost.
