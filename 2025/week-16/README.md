# Week 16, 2025 — Apr 14–19

> **Key Takeaway:** A week about scale going in two directions at once — smaller models gaining credibility (SLMs, O3/O4 Mini) while NVIDIA plants supercomputer manufacturing on US soil for geopolitical reasons. Salesforce's 20%-AI-generated code stat is the most concrete enterprise adoption number yet. OLMoTrace quietly addresses one of the field's most important unsolved problems: where does an AI's output actually come from?

---

## 📰 News

- **AI2 releases OLMoTrace** — System that traces LLM outputs back to their training data sources. Opens the door to explainability, trust, and addressing copyright/bias concerns at the source. Major step for AI transparency. *(Apr 2025)*
- **Salesforce: 20% of its code is now AI-generated** — Developers embracing AI for boilerplate and testing; freeing engineers for architecture and design work. Raises questions about the future demand for junior developers. *(Apr 2025)*
- **Small Language Models (SLMs) gain momentum** — Growing researcher interest in smaller, efficient models due to lower compute costs, energy efficiency, interpretability, and suitability for edge devices, healthcare, and privacy-sensitive contexts. *(Apr 2025)*
- **OpenAI essay: "Thinking With Images"** — Exploration of how multimodal models reason across images and language; how visual representations are built and used internally. Implications for education, creativity, and accessibility. *(Apr 2025)*
- **NVIDIA to manufacture AI supercomputers in the US** — Domestic production push driven by geopolitical tensions with China; aims for national AI independence and supply chain security. Chips framed as strategic national resource. *(Apr 2025)*

## 🚀 Model / Product Launches

- **[OpenAI O3 and O4 Mini](https://openai.com/o3)** — Lightweight models designed for speed, cost-efficiency, and task-specific deployment. Good performance at a fraction of GPT-4's size. Direct response to growing demand for smaller, cheaper, targeted LLMs. *(Apr 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- Salesforce's 20% AI-generated code figure is a landmark data point — not because it's surprising, but because it's a named, public company putting a real number on real usage. Expect others to follow.
- OLMoTrace is unglamorous but consequential: if it works at scale, it becomes the foundation for copyright accountability and bias auditing in LLMs. Exactly the kind of infrastructure the field needs.
- The SLM trend and O3/O4 Mini launch in the same week signal a genuine market bifurcation — frontier-scale for research and enterprise, small-scale for deployment and edge. Not every use case needs a 70B model.
- NVIDIA manufacturing in the US is a geopolitical move dressed as a supply chain decision. The chip-as-strategic-resource framing is going to define AI policy for the next decade.
