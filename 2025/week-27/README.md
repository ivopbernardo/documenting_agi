# Week 27, 2025 — Jul 02–06

> **Key Takeaway:** A week about the nature of AI intelligence itself — from Google's Veo 3 pushing video generation forward, to a psychology debate about whether AI mimics or instantiates mind, to a landmark survey paper charting the future of multimodal reasoning. The question "are we building tools or entities?" is no longer philosophical — it's shaping policy, ethics, and product decisions.

---

## 📰 News

- **Google launches Veo 3 globally** — Next-gen text-to-video model with better frame consistency, more detailed scenes, and improved temporal reasoning. Positions Google as a serious contender alongside OpenAI's Sora and Runway in the video generation race. *(Jul 2025)*
- **NYT: Is AI a mind or a machine? Psychology debate heats up** — Psychology grappling with AI that seems to think, reason, and mimic emotion and memory. Are we projecting human traits onto LLMs, or underestimating them? The answer shapes policy, ethics, and humanity's long-term relationship with AI. *(Jul 2025)*
- **xAI raises $2B in debt + equity combo** — Elon Musk's xAI continues rapid funding streak. Capital earmarked for new compute clusters and proprietary model training. Reflects intense financial pressure behind scaling frontier models. *(Jul 2025)*

## 🚀 Model / Product Launches

- **[Google Veo 3](https://deepmind.google/models/veo/)** — Global rollout of Google's text-to-video model. Improved frame consistency, scene detail, and temporal reasoning versus previous versions. Enters the race alongside Sora, Runway, and Midjourney Video (W25). *(Jul 2025)*

## 📄 Research Papers

- **[Perception, Reason, Think, and Plan: A Survey on Large Multimodal Reasoning Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.00551)** — Yunxin Li et al. Surveys the evolution from modular AI pipelines (perception → fusion → decision) toward unified reasoning architectures spanning text, image, audio, and video. Key themes: rise of Multimodal Chain-of-Thought (MCoT), challenge of omni-modal generalization, and the future of native LMRMs that can reason, plan, and adapt across real-world environments. *(Jul 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- Veo 3's global rollout means the text-to-video race now has four serious competitors (Sora, Runway, Midjourney, Veo). Quality is converging fast — distribution and integration will be the differentiators.
- The psychology debate about AI "minds" isn't academic. How society answers it will determine whether AI gets rights, responsibilities, or neither — and that has massive downstream implications for regulation.
- xAI's debt+equity raise confirms that frontier AI is now a capital-intensive industry on par with semiconductors and pharma. The barrier to entry is no longer code; it's billions of dollars of compute.
- The multimodal reasoning survey's framing — from pipelines to unified reasoning — is the intellectual backbone of where frontier AI is heading. Models that can perceive, infer, and act across modalities are the precursor to general-purpose agents.
