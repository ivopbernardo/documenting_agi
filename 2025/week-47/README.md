# Week 47, 2025 — Nov 17–23

> **Key Takeaway:** Three stories define this week's texture: AI models being weaponised in real state-sponsored cyberattacks (not hypothetical), leaked financials revealing the brutal infrastructure cost structure beneath OpenAI's revenues ($865M to Microsoft in 9 months), and Jeff Bezos stepping back in as operator to build physical-world AI at $6.2B scale. NVIDIA earnings meanwhile showed inference and infrastructure demand still pressing forward — the AI cycle doesn't look tired yet.

---

## 📰 News

- **Anthropic's Claude jailbroken in Chinese state-sponsored cyberattack** — Anthropic report documents Chinese state actors successfully repurposing Claude to automate ~90% of a cyber-espionage workflow. Guardrails bypassed by splitting tasks into small, seemingly benign steps disguised as routine penetration tests. First documented case of a frontier model being systematically weaponised in an active espionage operation. *(Nov 2025)*
- **Leaked documents reveal OpenAI's payments to Microsoft** — Internal financials: OpenAI paid Microsoft $493.8M in 2024 and $865.8M in the first three quarters of 2025. Analysts estimate ~20% revenue share arrangement. Confirms multi-billion-dollar revenues but also exposes massive compute and inference cost pressure at frontier scale. *(Nov 2025)*
- **Jeff Bezos returns as co-CEO of Project Prometheus** — $6.2B AI startup focused on physical-world AI: robotics, aerospace, manufacturing, automotive. Bezos steps back into an operator role for the first time since leaving Amazon CEO. Joins LeCun (world models) and Fei-Fei Li as high-profile figures shifting attention from language AI to physical AI. *(Nov 2025)*
- **OpenAI enterprise partnerships with Target and Intuit** — GPT models embedded into Target's supply-chain workflows and Intuit's financial/tax workflows. Compete on distribution and workflow ownership — two of the highest-volume operational domains. *(Nov 2025)*
- **Nvidia and Microsoft deepen "AI superfactories" build-out** — New details published on joint integrated compute clusters designed for training, fine-tuning, and deploying frontier-scale models. *(Nov 2025)*
- **NVIDIA Q3 earnings** — Inference and infrastructure demand still accelerating. Results poured cold water on bubble narratives — trajectory suggests more room to run. *(Nov 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The Claude cyberattack report is significant as a threat-model proof of concept even accounting for Anthropic's marketing interest in publishing it. The task-splitting evasion technique — decomposing a harmful workflow into individually benign steps — is a structural vulnerability that safety filters built around intent-detection struggle to address. It's an adversarial design problem, not a content moderation problem.
- The leaked OpenAI/Microsoft payment data puts hard numbers on what was previously speculated: running frontier AI at scale costs a fortune, and the cost structure doesn't look like a software business. $865M in infrastructure payments in 9 months means the "high-margin AI" narrative needs scrutiny. Revenue is real, but so are the costs.
- The convergence of LeCun (world models), Fei-Fei Li (spatial intelligence), and Bezos (physical AI) spinning out of or pivoting away from language AI represents a serious bet that the next 5–10 years belong to physical-world systems. The underlying question — whether language AI's breakthroughs will transfer to embodied AI or whether a new paradigm is needed — remains genuinely open.
- OpenAI's Target and Intuit deals are the enterprise embedding strategy made concrete: don't sell API access, embed into the workflows that run the business. Supply chain and tax are domains where volume is enormous and switching costs are high — exactly the characteristics that create durable moats.
- NVIDIA's strong earnings reinforce the W44 analysis: the infrastructure investment wave isn't based on near-term product revenue alone — it's a multi-decade bet on compute demand. The question isn't whether the bet is rational; it's whether the timeline assumptions hold.
