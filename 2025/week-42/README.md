# Week 42, 2025 — Oct 13–19

> **Key Takeaway:** AI is industrializing fast — Nvidia/Oracle tackle infrastructure, Salesforce and Adobe build enterprise layers, Anthropic and OpenAI expand into browsers and dev tools. But the consumer growth story has a crack in it: ChatGPT mobile DAUs are flattening, suggesting the first wave of mainstream adoption has plateaued. The next growth phase will come from embedding AI into workflows, not from convincing new users to try a chatbot.

---

## 📰 News

- **ChatGPT mobile growth slows** — Sensor Tower data shows downloads and DAUs decelerating after explosive early growth. Core user engagement remains strong, but saturation among early adopters, fewer "wow" updates, and rising competition from Gemini, Copilot, and Claude are cited as causes. *(Oct 2025)*
- **Nvidia and Oracle accelerate enterprise AI data processing** — Partnership combines Nvidia CUDA-X libraries and DGX Cloud with Oracle AI Vector Search and Autonomous Database. Focus on the three enterprise bottlenecks: data ingestion, transformation, and model training. Another potential case of circular investment between Big Tech players. *(Oct 2025)*

---

## 🚀 Model / Product Launches

- **OpenAI ChatGPT Atlas** — AI-native browser with ChatGPT built in. Launching on macOS first; Windows, iOS, and Android coming. Direct competition with Chrome and Perplexity Comet. OpenAI's play to own the browsing context layer. *(Oct 2025)*
- **Anthropic Claude Code (web)** — Browser-based development environment: real-time debugging, file management, local context memory. Claude as a true AI pair programmer in the browser, no IDE install required. *(Oct 2025)*
- **Salesforce Agentforce 360** — Enterprise AI layer unifying customer data, automation, and conversational interfaces across CRM, marketing, and support. Merges Einstein Copilot with an extensible agent framework. Slack repositioned as enterprise search; Gmail, Outlook, and Dropbox connectors planned for early 2026. *(Oct 2025)*
- **Adobe Foundry** — Service for enterprises to build custom generative AI models using their own data and brand guidelines. Integrates with Firefly and Creative Cloud. Adobe's move from creative tools company to AI infrastructure provider. *(Oct 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The ChatGPT growth slowdown is the most structurally important data point this week. Consumer AI tools that rely on novelty for adoption face a ceiling; the durable products will be those embedded so deeply into workflows that switching is costly. The browser play (Atlas, Comet) is exactly that bet.
- OpenAI and Anthropic both launching browsers/browser-adjacent dev tools in the same period is not a coincidence. The browser is the last interface layer neither controls. Whoever habituates users to their browser owns the default context window for every interaction.
- Salesforce repositioning Slack as enterprise search is a significant pivot. Slack as a communication tool competes with Teams; Slack as an AI-powered knowledge graph of organizational context competes with no one. That's the real strategic move buried in the Agentforce 360 announcement.
- Adobe Foundry targeting brand-aligned custom models is smart positioning. Enterprise AI adoption has been slowed partly by IP and brand-consistency concerns. Letting companies train on their own assets removes the two biggest objections at once.
- The Nvidia/Oracle partnership raises the same question as the Nvidia/OpenAI deal in W39: how much of Big Tech AI spending is genuinely additive vs. circular? If Nvidia invests in OpenAI and OpenAI buys Nvidia chips, and Oracle sells infrastructure that runs on Nvidia GPUs, the headline numbers overstate the net new capital entering the ecosystem.
