# Week 30, 2025 — Jul 21–27

> **Key Takeaway:** Consumer AI is exploding — 1.7B downloads and $1.87B in revenue in H1 2025 alone — while OpenAI's accidental indexing of private ChatGPT conversations showed that privacy expectations and AI convenience are on a collision course. ## 📰 News

- **OpenAI pulls ChatGPT conversation sharing feature after privacy blunder** — Opt-in feature that made ChatGPT conversations discoverable via Google accidentally indexed thousands of sensitive chats. OpenAI working with search providers to de-index. Signals that even opt-in features can backfire without robust user protections; privacy expectations around AI are tightening. *(Jul 2025)*
- **GenAI apps hit 1.7B downloads and $1.87B revenue in H1 2025** — Up 70% from late 2024; revenue doubled from previous period. ChatGPT led monetization outside China with users spending 16+ minutes/day. AI apps becoming daily tools, especially for younger mobile-first audiences. *(Jul 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- Ivo's reflection: "I've found myself using these tools so much that I'm willing to trade a bit of privacy for speed." The social media precedent suggests society will accept the privacy trade-off for AI convenience — the ChatGPT blunder is a stumble, not a reversal.
- 1.7B downloads in a half-year is a consumer adoption curve that rivals the early smartphone era. The monetization numbers ($1.87B) suggest this isn't just downloads — people are paying.
- "AI slop" — algorithmically generated low-quality content — is the dark flip side of the content explosion. The attention economy is about to face the same crisis that hit social media, but accelerated.
