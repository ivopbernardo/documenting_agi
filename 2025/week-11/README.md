# Week 11, 2025 — Mar 10–14

> **Key Takeaway:** A quieter week on model releases but rich on structural shifts: Google doubled down on Anthropic while running its own Gemini effort, expertise is being redefined from knowledge to judgment, and reinforcement learning is spreading from text to vision. The trend of data-efficient, reward-driven training (seen in DeepSeek R1, LIMO, and now Visual-RFT) is becoming a clear thread.

---

## 📰 News

- **Google expands investment in Anthropic** — Part of Google's strategy to stay at the AI frontier while diversifying beyond its own Gemini ecosystem. Raises questions about how a major investor coexists with a direct competitor in the LLM space. *(Mar 2025)*
- **Two new AI benchmarks target model fairness** — New benchmarks using diverse datasets and real-world applications to measure where AI systems fail at equitable decision-making. Structured approach to assessing and mitigating harmful outputs. *(Mar 2025)*
- **HBR: Gen AI reshapes the value of expertise** — Harvard Business Review piece argues expertise will shift from knowledge accumulation to judgment, problem framing, and AI-assisted decision-making. Professionals across industries need to rethink their roles. *(Mar 2025)*

## 📄 Research Papers

- **[Visual Reinforcement Fine-Tuning (Visual-RFT)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.01785)** — Extends Reinforcement Fine-Tuning (RFT) to vision-language models. Enables large models to learn effectively from limited data using verifiable reward mechanisms — no need for vast labeled datasets. Continues the trend of data-efficient, reward-driven training seen in DeepSeek R1 and LIMO. *(Mar 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- Google investing in Anthropic while building Gemini is a hedge, not a contradiction — but it creates an uncomfortable dynamic when Anthropic's safety positioning is part of its brand.
- Visual-RFT is the third data point (after DeepSeek R1's pure RL and LIMO's 817 samples) suggesting that reward-driven, data-efficient training is a genuine paradigm shift, not a one-off result. Worth watching as a thread.
- The expertise redefinition debate is going to accelerate: if AI handles knowledge retrieval, the premium shifts to judgment, context-setting, and knowing what questions to ask.
