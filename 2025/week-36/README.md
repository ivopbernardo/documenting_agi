# Week 36, 2025 — Sep 01–07

> **Key Takeaway:** Three forces converged this week: AI copyright law got its first concrete outcome (Anthropic's $1.5B settlement), capital markets doubled down on the AI race (Anthropic's $183B Series F), and product distribution became the new battleground (Google's Nano Banana image editor hit 10M users, OpenAI bought Statsig to industrialize experimentation). The hype curve may be flattening in perception, but the structural fights over law, money, and distribution are just beginning.

---

## 📰 News

- **Anthropic settles with authors for $1.5B over pirated training data** — Approximately $3,000 per pirated book. Anthropic must also delete the dataset. The case is specifically about piracy, not the broader legality of training on copyrighted works — that question remains unresolved. Apple was separately sued in California over similar claims. Expect more lawsuits against large model trainers. *(Sep 2025)*
- **OpenAI Collective Alignment update** — ~1,000 global perspectives gathered on model specifications. Feedback largely aligned with current rules, but disagreements pushed updates to safety and transparency guidelines. Public input shaping model behavior at scale. *(Sep 2025)*
- **GPT-5 vs GPT-4o blind test** — Third-party comparisons showed strong benchmark gains (94.6% vs 71% on math, 74.9% vs 30.8% on coding, 80% fewer hallucinations), but users voiced fatigue with "new model" hype and concerns about experience within the GPT-5 ecosystem. *(Sep 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The Anthropic copyright settlement is a precedent, but a narrow one. It resolves piracy liability, not the larger question of whether training on legally obtained copyrighted works requires compensation. That fight is still coming and will define the economics of future training runs.
- Anthropic raising $13B at a $183B post-money valuation while simultaneously settling a $1.5B lawsuit is the clearest illustration yet of the AI industry's detachment from normal risk calculus. Capital is essentially underwriting legal exposure.
- Google's Nano Banana reaching 10M users and 200M images in weeks shows that image editing — not chatbots — may be the consumer AI category that drives mass stickiness. Distribution through Gemini App gives Google a structural advantage here.
- OpenAI buying Statsig ($1.1B) to bring in a product-testing infrastructure startup signals the company is shifting from research-led to product-led growth. The Kevin Weil → VP of OpenAI for Science move reflects the same shift: science is now a product function.
- The Collective Alignment project is the right governance direction, but gathering 1,000 perspectives while deploying to hundreds of millions of users raises the question of whether "public input" is meaningful at that scale ratio.
