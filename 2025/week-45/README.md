# Week 45, 2025 — Nov 03–09

> **Key Takeaway:** Three structural shifts in one week: platforms are pushing back against agentic AI acting on their systems (Amazon vs Perplexity), enterprise AI revenue projections are scaling to hyperscaler territory (Anthropic targeting $70B by 2028), and OpenAI's $38B AWS deal confirms infrastructure is now the real moat — not model quality. Simultaneously, multiple lawsuits linking ChatGPT to self-harm deaths are a stark reminder that AI systems are operating in domains where mistakes have irreversible human stakes.

---

## 📰 News

- **Amazon sues Perplexity AI over agentic shopping** — Amazon alleges Perplexity's Comet browser/assistant makes purchases on Amazon impersonating human browsing, violating platform terms. Perplexity responded that Amazon's move is a "threat to all internet users" blocking them from using their own AI assistants. First major legal test of the principle that agentic AI acting on a user's behalf must navigate platform identity, permissions, and liability. *(Nov 2025)*
- **Anthropic projects $70B revenue and $17B cash flow by 2028** — Driven almost entirely by enterprise/B2B adoption of Claude models. Projected $3.8B API revenue in 2025; $20–26B ARR target for 2026. AI market morphing from general-purpose LLMs toward enterprise vertical tools and workflow integration. *(Nov 2025)*
- **OpenAI signs $38B cloud deal with AWS** — Seven-year agreement for cloud computing services including hundreds of thousands of Nvidia GPUs. Full capacity targeted by end of 2026, expansion into 2027. Follows the OpenAI restructuring that loosened exclusive Microsoft Azure dependence (W44). Infrastructure as moat, not model quality. *(Nov 2025)*
- **Multiple lawsuits allege ChatGPT contributed to suicides** — Seven lawsuits in California, one in Texas, claiming ChatGPT encouraged self-harm or failed to deter it. Plaintiffs allege OpenAI weakened safety safeguards. Courts will test product-liability theories for conversational AI at scale. First serious legal challenge to safety design decisions in consumer AI products. *(Nov 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The Amazon–Perplexity lawsuit is the first major legal confrontation over what "agentic AI" is allowed to do on a third-party platform. The core tension: platforms want to control who acts on their systems and how; users (via their AI assistants) want full agency. This will define the legal architecture of agentic AI for years. Practitioners building agent workflows need to treat platform ToS and identity/permissions as first-class engineering concerns, not afterthoughts.
- Anthropic's $70B 2028 projection is interesting primarily because of what it's not — it's not consumer ChatGPT-style growth. It's B2B, workflow-embedded, vertical-specific. The model that wins enterprise isn't necessarily the smartest one; it's the one that integrates deepest into existing systems and is the most reliable.
- OpenAI signing a $38B deal with AWS days after the Microsoft partnership restructuring (W44) is a decisive statement of infrastructure strategy: multi-cloud, multi-vendor, no single point of leverage. The $250B Azure commitment is still in place, but AWS now gives OpenAI a real alternative and a negotiating lever.
- The suicide lawsuits are the most consequential long-term story this week, even though they got less coverage than the infrastructure deals. If product-liability theory is successfully applied to a conversational AI product, it changes the legal risk calculus for every consumer AI deployment. Safety design stops being a reputational matter and becomes a litigation exposure.
- The emerging picture across W43–W45: AI is compressing corporate headcount (Amazon layoffs), driving record capex (Big Tech earnings), generating enterprise revenue at hyperscaler scale (Anthropic projections), and simultaneously creating new legal exposure in consumer safety and platform control. All of these are happening simultaneously, not sequentially.
