# Week 50, 2025 — Dec 08–14

> **Key Takeaway:** Enterprise AI adoption has entered an acceleration phase — OpenAI's State of Enterprise AI report shows median sector growth of 6x YoY, with frontier firms using AI 2x more intensely than median and completing entirely new task categories. The model race is running hot simultaneously: GPT-5.2, Devstral 2.0, Runway Gen-4.5, and DeepMind's Genie 3 (world model from video) all landed in the same week. The gap between leading and lagging organisations is widening fast.

## 📰 News

- **OpenAI hires Denise Dresser (former Slack CEO) as CRO** — Signals OpenAI's intense focus on monetising frontier models, scaling enterprise sales, and building long-term partnerships. Enterprise revenue becoming as strategically important as model research. *(Dec 2025)*
- **Instacart and OpenAI deepen partnership** — Integration of GPT models into shopping search and personalised recommendations. Another high-volume consumer platform embedding OpenAI into core product flows. *(Dec 2025)*
- **OpenAI State of Enterprise AI 2025 Report** — Key findings: ChatGPT Enterprise users save 40–60 minutes per active day; 75% of users complete new tasks they previously couldn't (coding, data analysis by non-technical teams); median sector grew 6x YoY; frontier firms generate 2x more messages per seat and 7x more messages to Custom GPTs than median; Australia and Brazil among fastest-growing international markets. Note: report published by OpenAI — interpret with appropriate scepticism. *(Dec 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The OpenAI enterprise report's most important finding is the adoption gap: frontier firms generate 7x more Custom GPT messages than the median. This is the W43 Wharton finding playing out in sharper relief — the gap between organisations that have genuinely integrated AI into workflows and those still experimenting is compounding, not closing.
- The 75% "new task completion" figure is notable: AI is not just making people faster at existing work, it's enabling non-technical people to do technical work (coding, data analysis). This is a longer-term disruption signal than productivity gains — it changes who can do what, not just how fast.
- Genie 3 learning world physics from unlabeled video is architecturally distinct from anything in the current LLM stack. It's the kind of generalisation that Sutskever's "end of scaling" speech (W49) was pointing toward — capabilities that require genuine environmental understanding, not pattern prediction at scale.
- Runway Gen-4.5 entering the video generation race at the same time as Sora 2 (W40) and YouTube's Veo 3 integration (W38) confirms that generative video is a legitimate product category, not just a research demo. Cinematic control and clip consistency are the right axes to compete on for professional media workflows.
- Denise Dresser's hire as CRO is the OpenAI equivalent of Apple hiring Subramanya (W48): bringing in someone who knows enterprise revenue at scale (Slack was a benchmark B2B SaaS product) to turn model leadership into durable commercial relationships. The pattern across both companies is the same — research leadership is no longer sufficient; you need enterprise GTM expertise.
