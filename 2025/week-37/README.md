# Week 37, 2025 — Sep 08–14

> **Key Takeaway:** Two data points this week deserve attention together: the OpenAI/NBER study of 1.5M+ real conversations shows AI is predominantly a decision-support tool, not an automation engine — while OpenAI simultaneously upgraded Codex to push deeper into autonomous software development workflows. The gap between how people actually use AI and what the labs are building toward is growing.

---

## 📰 News

- **OpenAI + NBER: largest ChatGPT usage study yet** — 1.5M+ conversations analyzed. ~30% work-related, ~70% personal. Most interactions fall under "asking and decision support," not pure automation. Baseline for tracking how AI embeds into daily life as models improve. *(Sep 2025)*
- **YouTube introduces generative AI tools for Shorts** — Powered by Google's Veo 3: text-to-video for Shorts, automated editing, remixing options. Aimed at speeding up creator workflows and gaining ground on TikTok and Instagram. *(Sep 2025)*

## 🚀 Model / Product Launches

- **OpenAI GPT-5-Codex and Codex enhancements** — Faster coding agents, integrated code review, stronger IDE/CLI integrations. Part of a broader push to make AI agents directly useful inside software development workflows. *(Sep 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The ChatGPT usage data (30% work, 70% personal; mostly "asking and decision support") is a useful corrective to both hype and doom narratives. AI is a decision assistant, not yet an autonomous agent, at population scale.
- YouTube's Veo 3 integration into Shorts is the clearest example of Google's vertical integration advantage: model → product → distribution in one stack. Standalone AI video tools can't compete with that flywheel.
- OpenAI's Codex upgrade is aimed at the 30% of work-related use. The bet is that software development is the category where autonomous agents can first prove out — tighter feedback loops, measurable correctness, lower stakes than, say, medical decisions.
