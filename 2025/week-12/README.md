# Week 12, 2025 — Mar 17–22

> **Key Takeaway:** A big infrastructure week: NVIDIA's Blackwell GPUs redefined the compute ceiling, OpenAI's Agent SDK moved AI from chatbot to autonomous enterprise actor, and Mistral proved again that open-source can punch above its weight. The creativity paper is a sleeper — AI-generated memes outperforming humans in humor and shareability is a data point that will matter in the coming IP and creative economy debates.

## 🚀 Model / Product Launches

- **[OpenAI Agent SDK](https://openai.com/index/new-tools-for-agentic-ai)** — Framework for building AI agents that perform tasks autonomously, integrate with enterprise systems, and make context-aware decisions. Positions OpenAI directly against Microsoft Copilot and Google AI Workflows in the enterprise automation space. *(Mar 2025)*
- **[Mistral new open-source model](https://mistral.ai)** — Outperforms GPT-4o Mini while using fewer parameters. Continues Mistral's pattern of efficient open-source models beating proprietary alternatives on benchmarks. *(Mar 2025)*
- **[NVIDIA Blackwell GPU architecture](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/data-center/blackwell/)** — Major leap in inference performance. Designed for large-scale AI model training and enterprise AI/robotics workloads. Introduced at GTC 2025. *(Mar 2025)*

## 📄 Research Papers

- **[Can AI Be Funny? Human-AI Meme Collaboration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.14484)** — Compared three groups: human-only, AI-assisted, and AI-only meme creators. Key findings: AI assistance increased idea volume but didn't improve quality in human collaboration; AI-generated memes outperformed humans in creativity, humor, and shareability; top funniest memes still came from humans alone; human-AI collaborations excelled in creativity and viral potential. *(Mar 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The Agent SDK is OpenAI's clearest move yet from consumer AI to enterprise infrastructure. The battleground is now workflow automation, not chat.
- NVIDIA's "AI Factories" framing is deliberate — positioning compute infrastructure as industrial output, not research tooling. It's a land-grab narrative.
- The meme paper result (AI > humans on humor metrics) will be cited heavily in future creative economy and IP debates. The finding that humans still produce the *funniest* memes but AI wins on *shareability* is a nuanced and uncomfortable split.
