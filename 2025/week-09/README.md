# Week 9, 2025 — Feb 24–28

> **Key Takeaway:** AI is expanding into new domains — journalism, workplace surveillance, code generation — while a GitHub Copilot security incident exposed the tension between AI convenience and data privacy. Karpathy's GPT explainer is a landmark education resource. The dual-use nature of AI (efficiency vs. control) is the defining tension of this period.

---

## 📰 News

- **Major newsrooms embrace AI** — Four leading newsrooms integrating ML tools for drafting stories, summarizing topics, fact-checking, and headline generation. Seen as a potential business model lifeline for a sector battered by the internet era. *(Feb 2025)*
- **GitHub Copilot can access private repositories** — TechCrunch reports thousands of repos marked private remain accessible via Copilot. Raises data privacy concerns about AI tools and their interaction with sensitive codebases. Highlights Microsoft's dual control over GitHub and Copilot. *(Feb 2025)*
- **AI surveillance in the workplace** — MIT Technology Review piece on companies deploying AI to monitor productivity metrics and communication patterns. Raises questions about privacy, worker autonomy, and the emerging "techno state." *(Feb 2025)*
- **GPT-4.5 unveiled by OpenAI** — Incremental update to the GPT series with improved performance and more nuanced understanding. Comes with a significant price increase. *(Feb 2025)*

## 📄 Research Papers

- **[S*: Test-Time Scaling for Code Generation](https://github.com/NovaSky-AI/SkyCode-S-star)** — NovaSky AI. First hybrid test-time scaling framework for code generation, combining sequential scaling with a novel input selection mechanism. Results: a 3B-parameter model outperforms GPT-4o-mini; top models reach near-SOTA on LiveCodeBench. Tested across 12 LLMs and reasoning frameworks. Key insight: non-reasoning models can exceed advanced systems with the right test-time strategy. *(Feb 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The GitHub Copilot privacy incident is a warning sign: as AI tools become deeply embedded in development workflows, the blast radius of a security lapse grows dramatically.
- AI in newsrooms could be journalism's survival mechanism — or accelerate its commoditization. The editorial judgment question remains open.
- Worker surveillance via AI is the quiet flip side of the productivity narrative. Efficiency gains and autonomy erosion often arrive together.
