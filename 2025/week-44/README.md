# Week 44, 2025 — Oct 27–Nov 02

> **Key Takeaway:** Big Tech's Q3 earnings delivered one message: AI spending is accelerating past any previous projection (Google $91–93B capex, Meta $70–72B, Microsoft $34.9B in a single quarter — a 74% YoY jump). Simultaneously, OpenAI formalised its for-profit conversion and rewrote its Microsoft partnership terms, including an AGI verification panel and expanded third-party rights. The capital and governance structures of the AI era are being set right now, at historic scale.

---

## 📰 News

- **OpenAI completes shift to for-profit company** — Microsoft holds a 27% stake post-restructuring. AGI will be declared only after verification by an independent expert panel. Sam Altman holds no equity in the new entity. Opens the door to large-scale capital raises and a potential IPO. *(Nov 2025)*
- **OpenAI and Microsoft announce new partnership terms** — Key changes: AGI declaration requires independent expert panel; Microsoft IP rights extended to 2032 including post-AGI models; Microsoft can now pursue AGI independently or with third parties; OpenAI contracted to purchase $250B of Azure services but Microsoft loses right of first refusal as compute provider; OpenAI can now release open-weight models; API products with third parties exclusive to Azure, non-API products can run on any cloud. *(Nov 2025)*
- **Big Tech Q3 earnings — AI dominates every line item:**
  - **Meta:** Revenue $51.24B (+26% YoY). Capex $70–72B for the year; CFO expects "notably larger" in 2026. Front-loading infrastructure for potential AI/superintelligence timelines. Some talent comp packages in the hundreds of millions. *(Nov 2025)*
  - **Google (Alphabet):** Revenue $102.3B (+33% YoY). Google Cloud $15.15B (+35%). Capex raised to $91–93B, up from $75B estimate. Gemini reached 650M MAUs (up from 450M). *(Nov 2025)*
  - **Microsoft:** Revenue $77B (+18% YoY). Cloud +26%. Quarterly capex $34.9B — a 74% YoY jump, nearly $5B above expectations. Sequential increases expected into FY26. *(Nov 2025)*
- **Zoom CEO: AI will cut the workweek** — Eric Yuan stated that AI will compress the five-day workweek by reducing meeting load, coordination work, and repetitive communication. Agents handle routine tasks; humans focus on oversight and decision-making. One of the most direct public statements from a major tech CEO framing AI as a structural shift in how society operates, not just a productivity boost. *(Nov 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The revised OpenAI–Microsoft terms deserve careful reading. The AGI verification panel is a governance mechanism that didn't exist before — it means "AGI" is now a legal and contractual trigger, not just a philosophical aspiration. Whoever controls that panel's composition and criteria holds significant power.
- Microsoft losing right of first refusal as OpenAI's compute provider while OpenAI commits to $250B in Azure purchases is a nuanced shift: the obligation is financial, not exclusive. OpenAI gains optionality; Microsoft gains revenue certainty. Both sides de-risk concentration.
- Google's Gemini reaching 650M MAUs in one quarter — up from 450M — is the single most important user-growth data point this week. That's 200M new monthly active users in roughly 90 days. The ChatGPT growth plateau noted in W42 contrasts sharply with Gemini's trajectory.
- The combined capex picture (Google $91B + Meta $72B + Microsoft $140B annualised) represents infrastructure investment at a scale that implies multi-decade demand assumptions. Either these bets pay off or this becomes the defining bubble of the 2020s — there's not much middle ground at those numbers.
- Claude for Finance is Anthropic's most concrete vertical move yet. The Excel integration is strategically sharp: it meets financial analysts exactly where they already work, rather than asking them to change their tool. Domain-specific embedding beats general-purpose chat for enterprise stickiness.
- Zoom's CEO making structural workweek predictions publicly is notable not for its novelty but for who's saying it. When a company whose revenue depends on meetings says meetings will shrink, it signals real belief in the direction — and real strategic risk-taking.
