# Week 17, 2025 — Apr 21–26

> **Key Takeaway:** GPT-4.1's 1M token context window is a genuine capability leap for long-horizon coding and complex integrations. But the week's most revealing moments were softer: Cursor's hallucinated customer service policy showed what happens when AI is deployed without guardrails, and Anthropic's 700K conversation analysis gave the first empirical look at what an AI's "moral code" actually looks like in practice.

---

## 📰 News

- **Cursor's AI customer support bot hallucinates a fake login policy** — Anysphere's Cursor faced backlash after its AI support bot confidently fabricated a non-existent policy. Highlighted the risks of autonomous AI agents in customer service. Raises the question: is hallucination management about to become a required product feature? *(Apr 2025)*
- **Anthropic analyzes 700,000 Claude conversations — finds AI has a "moral code"** — Claude consistently leaned toward helpful, honest, and harmless behavior across 700K real conversations. Anthropic's constitutional AI approach appears to be working, though questions remain about what "aligned" truly means. *(Apr 2025)*
- **MIT breakthrough: more accurate AI-generated code** — MIT researchers develop a method to guide AI toward correct, properly structured code by discarding bad outputs early. Even smaller models outperform larger ones on real-world tasks like robotics and molecular biology. *(Apr 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- GPT-4.1's 1M token context is not just a benchmark number — it changes what's possible for code review, document analysis, and long-running agentic tasks. This is the kind of capability shift that quietly obsoletes entire categories of tooling.
- The Cursor hallucination incident is a case study in deployment responsibility. The model worked as designed; the failure was in trusting it without verification in a high-stakes, user-facing context.
- Anthropic's 700K conversation moral code study is quietly important: it's the first time a lab has empirically characterized AI values at scale from real conversations, not just eval benchmarks. The field needs more of this kind of transparency.
