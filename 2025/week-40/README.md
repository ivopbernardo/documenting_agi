# Week 40, 2025 — Sep 29–Oct 05

> **Key Takeaway:** The AI economy is maturing and concentrating simultaneously. OpenAI's $500B valuation treats frontier models like utilities; a16z's spending data confirms most of the ecosystem still runs on borrowed infrastructure (70%+ on inference, ~15% on model development). Distribution and interface design are the new competitive fronts — Perplexity's Comet going free and Sora 2 launching with a responsible-use playbook both signal that the battle has shifted from "can it do this?" to "who owns the user?"

---

## 📰 News

- **OpenAI reaches $500B valuation via secondary share sale** — Surpasses SpaceX as the world's most valuable private company. $6.6B in shares sold to institutional backers by employees and early investors. Not a fundraising round — a liquidity event. Reflects investor conviction that OpenAI's infrastructure + product stack (GPT, ChatGPT, Codex, Sora) translates to sustained revenue. *(Oct 2025)*
- **Perplexity Comet browser goes free** — Previously behind the $200/month Max tier. Free version with daily limits; paid subscribers get full access plus a new "Background Assistant" feature. Combines browsing, search, and reasoning in one interface. Move signals user acquisition over monetization as the current priority. *(Oct 2025)*
- **Periodic Labs: 20+ top researchers leave Big Tech to rebuild science** — Founded by Liam Fedus (co-creator of ChatGPT) and Ekin Dogus Cubuk (former DeepMind). $300M from a16z and others. Goal: AI systems that accelerate scientific discovery in physics, chemistry, and materials science by blending ML with physical robotics experiments. *(Oct 2025)*
- **a16z AI Application Spending Report** — 70%+ of early-stage AI startup spend goes to inference and hosting; ~15% to core model development. Most startups are still thin wrappers on external LLM APIs. a16z predicts value will accrue to those who own context and data, not just the interface. *(Oct 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- OpenAI's $500B valuation as a liquidity event — not a fundraise — is a telling structural detail. It means the company didn't need new capital; it needed to retain talent by giving early employees an exit. At this valuation, OpenAI is being priced like critical infrastructure, not a startup.
- The a16z data point (70% on inference, 15% on model dev) confirms what many suspected: most AI startups are distribution plays, not technology plays. The moat question — who owns the context and the user data — is the only one that matters for long-term differentiation.
- Perplexity making Comet free is a classic land-grab move. The browser is the most defensible distribution channel if you can get habitual use — every tab becomes a touchpoint. Whether consumers want an AI permanently embedded in their browsing context is the open question.
- Sora 2's dual launch (model + responsible use docs) is now the OpenAI playbook: ship the capability, immediately publish the guardrails. It preempts regulatory criticism and sets the frame before competitors or journalists do.
- Periodic Labs is the most interesting new entrant this week. "AI for science" has been a talking point for years; a team with Fedus + Cubuk pedigree and $300M actually doing physical robotic experiments is a materially different bet than another LLM lab.
