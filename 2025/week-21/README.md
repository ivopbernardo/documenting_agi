# Week 21, 2025 — May 19–24

> **Key Takeaway:** AI's infrastructure footprint is becoming impossible to ignore — data center electricity projected to triple in the US by 2030, with OpenAI building a 1.2 GW facility in Texas. Meanwhile, the product layer is maturing fast: Claude got web search, OpenAI released a healthcare benchmark, and agentic AI is already reshaping real work. The bottleneck is no longer models — it's energy and governance.

---

## 📰 News

- **AI data center power consumption projected to triple in the US by 2030** — Global data center electricity consumption on track to double by 2030; US share could jump from 4% to 12% of all electricity used. AI is an infrastructure revolution, not just a software one. Risks becoming a major bottleneck for sustainable AI growth. *(May 2025)*
- **OpenAI announces data centers in Texas (1.2 GW Stargate) and UAE (200 MW by 2026)** — Massive compute infrastructure investments to future-proof model scaling. Part of the broader AI energy/infrastructure build-out. *(May 2025)*
- **HBR: Agentic AI already reshaping the workforce** — AI agents with goals, reasoning, and independent action are automating and augmenting roles simultaneously. The value of "being human" at work is being redefined in real time. *(May 2025)*
- **GPT Store as platform strategy** — OpenAI's GPT Store positioned not just as a feature but as the foundation of a platform play — developers building specialized GPTs for tutoring, contract review, and more. Parallels to Apple's App Store model. *(May 2025)*

## 🚀 Model / Product Launches

- **[Claude Web Search API (Anthropic)](https://anthropic.com/news/claude-search)** — Claude gains real-time web access. Brings Anthropic into direct competition with Google Search, ChatGPT browsing, and Perplexity AI. Tests whether AI models can displace traditional search at scale. *(May 2025)*
- **[HealthBench (OpenAI)](https://openai.com/index/healthbench)** — Benchmark built with doctors to evaluate AI in medical settings. 5,000+ simulated health conversations with detailed rubrics. Step toward transparency and accountability for AI in healthcare. *(May 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The energy story is the most underreported structural constraint on AI development. A field that can't scale compute sustainably can't scale AI — and 12% of US electricity is not a rounding error.
- Claude with web search is the clearest test yet of whether Google's search moat holds. If users prefer conversational retrieval over ranked links, the advertising model that funds most of the internet is at risk.
- HealthBench built with practicing doctors is the right approach — domain experts co-designing benchmarks rather than ML researchers guessing at real-world requirements. Should become standard practice across high-stakes domains.
- The GPT Store platform analogy to Apple's App Store is compelling but the key question is network effects: does a GPT marketplace create the same developer/user flywheel that made the App Store defensible?
