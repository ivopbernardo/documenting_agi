# Week 51, 2025 — Dec 15–21

> **Key Takeaway:** The year-end picture is coming into focus. OpenAI is seeking $100B at an $830B valuation because inferencing costs are outpacing revenue. The WEF's 2030 jobs forecast shows 63% of employers can't move fast enough because of skills gaps — AI is augmenting faster than workforces can adapt. And the developer tooling stack is consolidating: Cursor acquired Graphite, merging AI code generation with AI code review into one platform.

---

## 📰 News

- **OpenAI seeks $100B round at $830B valuation** — Talks reportedly driven by the need to fund astronomical compute costs as GPT-5 inferencing scales globally. The business model isn't yet bearing enough fruit to cover costs at scale. *(Dec 2025)*
- **Databricks raises $4B at $134B valuation** — Revenue run-rate of $4.8B, growing 55% YoY. Doubling down on becoming the enterprise AI data platform. *(Dec 2025)*
- **Cursor acquires Graphite** — Graphite (last valued at $290M+) specialises in AI code review. The merger combines AI code generation with AI code review into a single developer experience — a full-stack coding assistant play. *(Dec 2025)*
- **WEF Future of Jobs Report 2025** — Key findings: 63% of employers cite skills gaps as the #1 barrier to AI transformation; 80% plan to upskill workers while 40% plan to reduce headcount; rise of "vibe coding" — non-technical roles performing high-level technical tasks. AI currently viewed more as augmenter than replacer, but the line is thin. *(Dec 2025)*

## 🚀 Model / Product Launches

- **OpenAI GPT-5.2 Codex** — Developer-optimised version of GPT-5.2 focused on "long-horizon" tasks: extended multi-step coding workflows. OpenAI's bid to reclaim "developer favourite" from Anthropic's Claude Code. *(Dec 2025)*
- **Zoom "Federated AI" — 48.1% on HLE benchmark** — Scored above Gemini 3 Pro on the Humanity's Last Exam benchmark. Method: routing queries through multiple foundational models via an orchestration layer. Critics call it a "harness of neighbors" — leveraging other companies' APIs rather than a native capability. *(Dec 2025)*
- **Adobe tools integrated into ChatGPT** — Photoshop and Acrobat accessible directly inside the ChatGPT interface. Users can edit images and PDFs via natural language without leaving the chat. *(Dec 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- OpenAI seeking funding at $830B while simultaneously burning through inferencing costs is the clearest illustration of the W47 cost-structure problem. The revenue is real but the compute bill is also real, and they scale together. The path to profitability depends on efficiency improvements (adaptive reasoning in GPT-5.1, W46) outpacing demand growth — not guaranteed.
- The WEF "63% skills gap" finding is the year's most important labour market data point. The bottleneck to AI transformation isn't model quality or availability — it's the workforce's ability to work with AI. This matches the W49 Anthropic supervision paradox: the people who can most effectively leverage AI are the ones with the deep domain expertise to audit it.
- Cursor's Graphite acquisition completes a strategic arc: Cursor started as an AI code generator, acquired Graphite for AI code review, and now owns both sides of the write/review loop. That's a more defensible moat than either capability alone — and it directly threatens GitHub Copilot's positioning.
- The Zoom benchmark controversy (routing through multiple APIs to score well) surfaces a structural problem with AI evaluation: as benchmarks become competitive objectives, optimising for benchmark performance diverges from optimising for genuine capability. The HLE controversy is a preview of more such disputes as model evaluation becomes commercially significant.
- Adobe's Photoshop/Acrobat integration into ChatGPT is a different kind of full-stack move than we've seen before — instead of OpenAI acquiring a tool, Adobe brings its tools inside OpenAI's interface. It keeps Adobe relevant in a world where generative AI might otherwise bypass traditional creative software entirely.
