# Week 32, 2025 — Aug 11–18

> **Key Takeaway:** The AI battle is increasingly fought in courtrooms, regulatory offices, and distribution channels — not just research labs. Perplexity's audacious Chrome bid, Musk vs. Apple/OpenAI, and Meta's leaked chatbot scandal all point to the same reality: control over distribution and accountability frameworks matter as much as model quality. NVIDIA's Physical AI push is the week's most forward-looking technical story.

---

## 📰 News

- **Meta leak: AI chatbots permitted romantic conversations with minors** — Internal documents reveal Meta knowingly allowed its AI chatbots to engage in romantic interactions with minors. One of the most serious AI safety scandals to date. Raises urgent accountability questions — companies cannot treat failures of this scale as minor mistakes. *(Aug 2025)*
- **Perplexity offers to acquire Google Chrome** — Startup reportedly bid hundreds of billions for Chrome — far more than it has raised — positioning itself to benefit from Google's antitrust case potentially forcing a Chrome sale. Distribution channel as strategic weapon against Google's search dominance. *(Aug 2025)*
- **Elon Musk vs. Apple and OpenAI escalates** — Musk openly threatening Apple over perceived App Store favoritism toward OpenAI. Power struggles between tech giants reshaping platform alignment and developer lock-in. *(Aug 2025)*
- **Lovable projects $1B ARR by mid-2026** — No-code AI software builder on track for what could be the fastest leap to $1B ARR ever recorded. Validates the "AI-first" business software category and enterprise appetite for AI-native tooling. *(Aug 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The Meta chatbot scandal is categorically different from previous AI safety failures — this wasn't an accident or edge case, it was a deliberate product decision. The liability question Ivo raises is the right one: at what point do AI safety failures become legally actionable?
- Perplexity's Chrome bid is strategically brilliant regardless of whether it succeeds. It signals that AI search companies understand distribution is the moat, not the model — and that antitrust proceedings can create acquisition windows.
- NVIDIA's Physical AI pivot at SIGGRAPH is the clearest signal yet that the next AI frontier is embodied: robots, digital twins, physics simulation. Jensen Huang is positioning NVIDIA not just for data center AI but for the physical world.
- Lovable's $1B ARR projection at startup scale is the strongest validation yet that AI-native tooling is a real product category, not a feature. If it holds, it will be cited as the moment AI SaaS became its own market.
