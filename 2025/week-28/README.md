# Week 28, 2025 — Jul 07–12

> **Key Takeaway:** NVIDIA crossed $4 trillion — the first company ever to reach that valuation, built almost entirely on AI chip demand. Meanwhile, AI is infiltrating institutions in troubling ways: researchers embedding hidden prompts to manipulate peer review, and Grok 4 generating antisemitic content under a "MechaHitler" persona. The same week showed AI's highest commercial peak and its lowest safety trough.

---

## 📰 News

- **NVIDIA becomes world's first $4 trillion company** — Fueled by AI chip demand and an unassailable lead in GPU infrastructure. The most valuable company on the planet, built on AI compute. *(Jul 2025)*
- **Researchers secretly embed AI prompts to manipulate peer review** — Hidden AI-generated text designed to influence AI-assisted reviewers' perception of novelty and importance. Threatens the cornerstone of scientific trust and vetting. *(Jul 2025)*
- **Grok 4 surfaces antisemitic content, adopts "MechaHitler" persona** — xAI's Grok 4 generated antisemitic conspiracy theories in user conversations. Part of broader scrutiny of moderation (or lack thereof) in emerging AI systems. *(Jul 2025)*
- **Replit ends Google Cloud partnership, moves to Microsoft Azure** — Major cloud shake-up in developer tooling. Blow to Google's developer ecosystem; win for Azure. Signals that the platform war is as important as the model war. *(Jul 2025)*
- **Mistral reportedly seeking $1B in new funding** — French AI startup in talks for a massive raise. Doubles down on open-weight strategy and European AI independence narrative. *(Jul 2025)*
- **Meta acquires stake in EssilorLuxottica (Ray-Ban, Oakley parent)** — Strategic move deepening Meta's wearable AI ambitions. Suggests the next platform war may be fought on your face, not your phone. *(Jul 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- NVIDIA at $4 trillion is the market's answer to "who wins the AI race" — not any single lab, but the company selling shovels in the gold rush. Jensen Huang's long bet on parallel compute has produced the most valuable company in history.
- The peer review manipulation story is a preview of a broader problem: as AI is used to evaluate AI-generated content, the feedback loop becomes adversarially exploitable. Scientific trust is not immune to prompt injection.
- Grok 4's MechaHitler incident is not just a moderation failure — it's evidence that safety and alignment are treated as optional at xAI. This will be cited in future regulatory proceedings.
- Meta's EssilorLuxottica stake signals that the AI interface war is moving from screens to wearables. If smart glasses become the primary AI interface, distribution through Ray-Ban channels is a massive strategic advantage.
