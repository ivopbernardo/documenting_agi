# Week 22, 2025 — May 26–31

> **Key Takeaway:** Google had a landmark week — Gemini 2.5 at I/O, AI Mode rolling out in US Search, and new DeepMind security safeguards. But the most consequential story may be quieter: Google's own research paper exposing why enterprise RAG systems fail. Meanwhile, Claude got voice mode and HBR asked the question the whole field is circling: can AI agents actually be trusted?

---

## 📰 News

- **Google launches "AI Mode" in US Search** — Gemini-generated overviews now appear above traditional search results. Redefines the internet's front page; threatens SEO, publishers, and Google's own ad-driven revenue model. Not a UI tweak — a structural shift in how information is accessed. *(May 2025)*
- **Claude Voice Mode launched** — Anthropic releases voice interaction for Claude: fast, expressive, safety-focused (responds only to its user, ignores background voices). Brings Claude into direct competition with ChatGPT's multimodal offering. *(May 2025)*
- **Google DeepMind unveils Gemini security upgrades** — New suite: better content filters, adversarial training, proactive misuse detection, and improved steerability for users and organizations. Signals security-by-design becoming a competitive feature, not an afterthought. *(May 2025)*
- **HBR: Can AI agents be trusted?** — Explores technical and social dimensions of agent trust: value alignment, transparency, human oversight. Argues that performance alone is not sufficient for agentic AI adoption. *(May 2025)*

## 📄 Research Papers

- **[Sufficient Context: A New Lens on RAG Systems (Google Research)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.06719)** — Identifies a critical flaw in enterprise RAG: insufficient context in retrieved documents. Proposes a framework to ensure retrieved content is relevant and usable, based on likelihood of sufficient context. Could significantly improve reliability of enterprise AI deployments. *(May 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- Google Search going AI-first is a bigger structural event than any model release this year. The ad-driven link economy that has funded the internet for 25 years is being disrupted from within by its creator.
- Claude's voice mode arriving weeks after it was first rumored (W13) shows how fast Anthropic is moving on multimodal. Voice is the natural next frontier — it lowers the barrier to AI interaction dramatically.
- Google's RAG paper is candid self-criticism from the field's most deployed AI infrastructure company. If enterprise RAG systems are failing at context retrieval, most deployed AI products are less reliable than their users assume.
- The trust paper is the meta-question of the agentic era: capability without trust is a liability, not an asset. As agents get more autonomous, the social contract around oversight will need to be formalized.
