# Week 26, 2025 — Jun 23–Jul 01

> **Key Takeaway:** The most consequential week for AI law so far: US courts ruled that training AI on copyrighted books is "fair use" for both Meta and Anthropic. If upheld, this precedent will define how the entire industry operates. Meanwhile, the field's evaluation crisis deepened — we're building increasingly powerful systems without reliable ways to measure what they actually understand. AI is now being shaped as much in courtrooms and talent markets as in labs.

---

## 📰 News

- **Meta and Anthropic win copyright lawsuits — AI training ruled "fair use"** — Two landmark US rulings found that training AI models on copyrighted books without author permission does not breach copyright law. Could set the foundational legal precedent for AI data usage in the US. If upheld, allows AI labs to continue using massive datasets without individual licensing. Significant implications for creators, publishers, and the economics of AI training data. *(Jul 2025)*
- **Meta hires leading OpenAI researcher to work on reasoning models** — Talent offensive targeting OpenAI's reasoning research. Signals that the AGI race is now fought on three fronts: compute, data, and human expertise. *(Jul 2025)*
- **MIT Technology Review: AI's evaluation crisis is getting worse** — Existing benchmarks are outdated, gameable, and fail to measure genuine understanding, reasoning, or alignment. Without solid evaluations, overhyping models and underestimating risks becomes systematic — especially as AI enters critical domains. *(Jul 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- The "fair use" rulings are the most important legal event in AI history so far. They don't just affect Meta and Anthropic — they potentially green-light the entire industry's training data practices. The O'Reilly investigation (W14) may now have a legal answer, even if not an ethical one.
- Meta poaching OpenAI's reasoning researcher is a direct strike at OpenAI's most defensible moat. Reasoning capability — not raw scale — is the current frontier, and talent is the bottleneck.
- The evaluation crisis is the field's most dangerous blind spot. If we can't measure what models actually understand, every capability claim and safety assurance is built on shaky ground. This problem compounds as models become more powerful.
- AI is now contested terrain across four dimensions simultaneously: model capability, legal framework, talent acquisition, and measurement validity. Labs that can win on all four will define the next phase.
