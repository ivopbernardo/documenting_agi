# Week 24, 2025 — Jun 09–14

> **Key Takeaway:** The biggest AI capital allocation story of the year so far: Meta committed $14.3B to Scale AI for a dedicated superintelligence lab. Simultaneously, Europe made its most credible bid yet for AI independence — Mistral launched a sovereign cloud platform backed by Microsoft and NVIDIA, while Jensen Huang outlined a roadmap for Europe as a third AI superpower. The US-China AI decoupling is creating a strategic opening that Europe is finally moving to fill.

---

## 📰 News

- **Meta invests $14.3 billion in Scale AI, launches superintelligence lab** — One of the largest single AI infrastructure bets to date. Meta believes scalable infrastructure and high-quality data labeling are the critical path to AGI. Could reshape the competitive landscape if it accelerates frontier model training. *(Jun 2025)*
- **NVIDIA CEO unveils Europe AI blueprint at GTC Paris** — Jensen Huang outlines strategy to make Europe a central AI player: regional data centers, AI research hubs, cross-border university and tech partnerships. Positions Europe as a potential third pillar in the global AI race as US-China tensions grow. *(Jun 2025)*

## 🚀 Model / Product Launches

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- Meta's $14.3B Scale AI bet is a statement about what they believe AGI requires: not just model architecture, but industrial-scale data quality infrastructure. If correct, data labeling becomes a strategic moat, not a commodity service.
- Mistral's European cloud + Microsoft backing is a nuanced move — it keeps European AI within a familiar enterprise stack while asserting data sovereignty. The real test is whether European enterprises choose it over AWS/Azure for reasons beyond compliance.
- NVIDIA supporting European AI independence while decoupling from China (W23) is a consistent strategy: replace one major market with many smaller sovereign ones. Jensen Huang is playing geopolitical chess.
- The combination of Mistral's cloud, NVIDIA's investments, and EU regulatory pressure could genuinely make Europe a third AI superpower — not in frontier model research, but in deployment infrastructure and governance standards.
