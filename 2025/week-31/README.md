# Week 31, 2025 — Jul 28–Aug 03

> **Key Takeaway:** Anthropic quietly overtook OpenAI in enterprise adoption — a reversal from 2023 when OpenAI had a 2x lead. In the same week, Zuckerberg announced Meta Superintelligence Labs and framed AGI as a race Meta intends to win. The enterprise market and the superintelligence race are now two separate games being played simultaneously, and different companies are leading each one.

---

## 📰 News

- **Anthropic overtakes OpenAI in enterprise AI adoption** — Menlo Ventures report: Anthropic at 32% enterprise LLM share vs. OpenAI's 25%. In developer/code workloads, gap widens: Anthropic 42%, OpenAI 21%. Complete reversal from 2023 when OpenAI had a 2x lead. Anthropic's alignment focus and coding tool specialization are paying off in enterprise. *(Aug 2025)*
- **Zuckerberg announces Meta Superintelligence Labs** — Formal launch of a dedicated superintelligence research lab. Recruited Alexandr Wang; $14.3B Scale AI investment (49% stake) provides the data infrastructure. Zuckerberg frames AGI as "in reach" and a race Meta intends to lead with a personal empowerment angle, not just automation. *(Aug 2025)*

## 💬 Opinion & Analysis

- Anthropic leading enterprise adoption is the most surprising competitive shift of 2025 so far. OpenAI's brand dominance in consumer didn't translate to enterprise — trust, safety positioning, and code-focused tooling won there.
- Ivo's reflection on Meta's superintelligence bet: "One of these companies will eventually build a system that can improve itself, but what happens then? It's more like we can create a parallel slack society, à la Wall-E." The anxiety is real and the question is unanswered.
- Meta Superintelligence Labs is significant not just as a funding announcement but as a framing shift — Zuckerberg is publicly committing to AGI as a goal, not a byproduct. That changes how the company is held accountable.
- The Anthropic vs. OpenAI enterprise battle will be won on developer experience, trust, and output reliability — not model benchmarks. The coding workload dominance (42% vs 21%) is the clearest signal of where enterprise value is being created.
